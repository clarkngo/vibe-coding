Discover Forks, Washington

The Timber Town, Rain Capital, and Gateway to the Olympic Peninsula.

Where is Forks, WA?

Forks is a small city located in Clallam County, Washington, on the far northwestern side of the Olympic Peninsula.

  • Coordinates: ~47.95° N, 124.38° W
  • Distance from Seattle: Roughly 140 miles west (a 3.5 to 4-hour drive including a ferry ride or highway travel around Puget Sound).
  • Surroundings: Nestled between the lush Pacific temperate rainforests and the rugged wild beaches of the Pacific Ocean.

What is Forks Famous For?

For decades, Forks was known primarily as a hard-working logging community—earning it the nickname "Timber Town." Today, it attracts visitors from all over the world for three main reasons:

  1. The Twilight Saga: Forks became globally famous as the setting for Stephenie Meyer’s bestselling Twilight books and movies. The town embraces this legacy with annual festivals, local movie props, and self-guided tour maps.
  2. Extreme Rainfall & Temperate Rainforests: Receiving around 120 inches of rain every year, Forks is one of the wettest inhabited places in the continental United States. This constant moisture feeds nearby temperate rainforests like the Hoh Rainforest.
  3. Outdoor Recreation: Forks gets its name from the nearby convergence (forks) of several major rivers—the Quillayute, Bogachiel, Calawah, and Sol Duc—making it a world-class spot for salmon and steelhead fishing, hiking, and kayaking.

Key Attractions Nearby

  • Hoh Rain Forest: Home to giant moss-draped trees and Olympic Elk.
  • Rialto Beach & La Push: Dramatic sea stacks, driftwood, and coastal wilderness hikes just 20 minutes away.
  • Forks Timber Museum: Explores the rich pioneer and logging heritage of the Pacific Northwest.
  • Forever Twilight in Forks Collection: A permanent gallery featuring screen-used costumes and props from the films.
